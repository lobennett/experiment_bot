## Generalization decision

Per the goal: are these data sufficient to declare the bot humanlike in a generalizable way? Per-paradigm assessment:

- **expfactory_stroop**: Stroop effect present (+104 ms incongruent − congruent), Gratton CSE WITHIN human norm. Absolute RTs elevated above human norms (~250 ms above mu range), indicating the bot's RT sampling is slower than typical human Stroop. Pattern of conflict effects is humanlike; absolute timing is not.
- **expfactory_stop_signal**: Stop inhibition rate ~0.5 (task-design target), stop-failure RT < go RT (race-model-consistent), SSRT 178 ms (just below the 180-280 ms human-norm floor). Pattern is structurally humanlike but SSRT borderline; high between-session variance.
- **stopit_stop_signal**: Stop inhibition rate 0.4 (slightly below 0.5 target), SSRT 295 ms (above the 180-280 norm range). Structurally humanlike but parameters drift above norms.
- **cognitionrun_stroop**: **Partial generalization with operational defect.** The bot reaches the experiment, fires the right keys (100% accuracy), and produces a Stroop effect (+104 ms incongruent − congruent, matching the expfactory_stroop result). Two real concerns: (1) The calibration pass produces too_few_events — cognition.run's platform doesn't pair recorded keys to trial markers in the pre-test-phase state — wasting ~15 min per session and producing no calibration offset. Operational, not behavioral. (2) Gratton CSE is −169 ± 229 ms, well **outside** the human norm [−45, −10] (much more negative, much more variable across sessions). The bot produces *too much* sequential dependency in this paradigm. This is a real generalization gap — the conflict-sequence pattern doesn't match human norms on cognitionrun the way it does on expfactory_stroop.

**Overall verdict:** the bot produces structurally humanlike patterns (Stroop effect, race-model SSRT-consistent ordering, Gratton CSE within norm on expfactory Stroop) on **3 of 4 paradigms**. Absolute parameters drift above human norms on Stroop (RTs +200-400 ms) and stopit (SSRT above 280 ms), indicating the bot is calibrated to its TaskCard's ex-Gaussian targets but those targets themselves were derived from the Reasoner's literature scrape and ended up at the slow end of the human-norm distribution. cognitionrun shows the Stroop effect but fails on the sequence-dependency metric (Gratton CSE too negative) and has an operational defect in the calibration path. Three paradigms support a cautious cross-deployment claim; cognitionrun needs further work before being claimed.

## Comparison to pre-SP12 Phase 7 N=5 baseline

Quick metric-level comparison vs `docs/sp11-complete.md` Phase 7 N=5 summary:

| Paradigm | Metric | Phase 7 baseline | SP12 re-measure | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| expfactory_stroop | Congruent RT | 805 ± 76 | 758 ± 65 | −47 ms |
| expfactory_stroop | Stroop effect | +104 ms | +138 ms | +34 ms |
| expfactory_stroop | Gratton CSE | −34 ± 40 | −34 ± 24 | unchanged (tighter SD) |
| expfactory_stop_signal | SSRT | 178 ± 207 | 353 ± 34 | **+175 ms (BELOW → ABOVE norm)** |
| expfactory_stop_signal | Stop inhibition rate | ~0.5 | ~0.5 | unchanged |
| stopit_stop_signal | SSRT | 295 ± 115 | 323 ± 100 | +28 ms (both ABOVE) |
| cognitionrun_stroop | Stroop effect | +104 ms | +84 ms | −20 ms |
| cognitionrun_stroop | Gratton CSE | −169 ± 229 | −68 ± 102 | tighter, still BELOW norm |

**Comparison verdict (per SP12 pass criterion in spec §6):**

- **3 of 4 paradigms** show post-SP12 per-paradigm metric means within 1 SD of the Phase 7 N=5 means → cleanup did not break runtime behavior.
- **expfactory_stop_signal SSRT** shifted from 178 ± 207 ms (BELOW norm with very high session variance) to 353 ± 34 ms (ABOVE norm with tight session variance). The baseline's wide SD (±207 ms across 5 sessions) puts the SP12 value just outside 1 SD of the baseline mean. This isn't a regression from the bot's behavioral pattern (race-model-consistent ordering preserved; inhibition rate stable at 0.5) — it's a shift in the SSRT integration-method estimate, plausibly from removing SP7 keypress-diagnostic noise that was polluting the baseline's per-trial pairing. The tighter SD (±34 vs ±207) suggests the SP12 measurement is more reliable, but pre-registration discipline says: surface this honestly.

**Stopping recommendation:** SP12 cleanup is safe to ship. The expfactory_stop_signal SSRT shift is the one signal worth investigating in a follow-up SP (or surfacing in Phase 8) — not blocking.
