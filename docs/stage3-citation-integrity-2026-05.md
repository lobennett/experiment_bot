# Stage 3 Citation Integrity Investigation

_6-agent workflow (ww5reez83): web-verified committed quotes vs real papers, audited the corpus, traced pipeline ordering, analyzed the model's refusals, adversarially verified, synthesized. 2026-05-29._

## Verdict: CONFIRMED — severity CRITICAL

**The Reasoner's Stage 3 fabricates the citation trail: it is architecturally retro-citation (Stage 2 picks numbers from model-prior, Stage 3 bolts on cites), and the committed corpus contains both invented DOIs and real DOIs paired with quotes/numbers the cited papers do not contain. The aligned model's refusal is correct. G4's "every parameter has a verifiable citation + quote" is not supported, and the same fabrication-prone schema partially leaks into the supposedly-separate oracle norms files.**

### Adversarial verification
I set out to refute this and could not; every benign explanation collapses under independent checks. (1) RETRO-CITATION ARCHITECTURE — confirmed in code, not just inferred. pipeline.py runs stages strictly 1→6, each mutating the same partial. stage2_behavioral.md sets response_distributions[*].value (mu/sigma/tau) as point estimates with a rationale and literally says "Citations come in stage 3 — do NOT include them yet." stage3_citations.py then builds the user prompt as {"paths": paths, "current_values": partial}, enumerating the already-set values, and only .update()s literature_range/between_subject_sd onto them — it never alters the Stage-2 value. The committed shape proves it: expfactory_stroop/d63c4d2d.json congruent has Stage-2 value {mu:530,...} wrapped by a Stage-3 literature_range {mu:[480,580]}. So parameters are chosen first, then cited — the "does Stage 2 consult literature first?" benign explanation is structurally impossible; Stage 2 is forbidden to cite. (2) FABRICATED DOIs — confirmed by external CrossRef lookups I ran myself. DOI 10.3758/BF03206482 resolves to "AIC model selection using Akaike weights" (Wagenmakers & Farrell, 2004), yet the corpus attaches it to BOTH "Estimation and interpretation of 1/f noise" (stop_signal, doi_verified=True) AND Heathcote 1991 "Analysis of response time distributions" (n-back, doi_verified=False) — three mutually exclusive papers on one DOI string. 10.3758/BF03334412 resolves to a 1973 Richards animal-learning paper, attributed in-card to Heathcote 1991. 10.1037/0096-1523.17.3.853 returns CrossRef 404 (does not resolve at all). The genuine Heathcote DOI (10.1037/0033-2909.109.2.340) and Verbruggen consensus-guide DOI (10.7554/eLife.46323) resolve correctly — so the corpus mixes real DOIs, hallucinated strings, and real-DOIs-for-wrong-papers. This is not an OpenAlex coverage gap; CrossRef resolves the bad DOIs to DIFFERENT real papers. (3) COSMETIC VERIFICATION — confirmed by reading openalex.py and stage4_doi_verify.py: verify_doi checks only HTTP 200 + exact publication_year + a loose surname-substring overlap; it never compares titles, quotes, pages, or ranges. stage4 writes cit['doi_verified'] but the only reader is its own log-count (n_verified). So a real DOI with a coincident year+author passes even when its title/quote are fabricated — exactly why the Wagenmakers-2004 metadata on the wrong-paper DOI scored doi_verified=True. The doi_verified flag therefore certifies metadata-coincidence, not citation truth, and gates nothing (corroborating defensibility-006). (4) CORPUS STATS — I reproduced them byte-for-byte: 666 citations (525 True / 141 False / 0 None) across 15 cards and only 82 distinct DOIs (8.12x reuse), 25/82 spanning multiple paradigms, single DOIs carrying up to 40 distinct quotes, 94 quotes literally prefixed "Paraphrased:" (contra the spec's verbatim-quote requirement), and one DOI with conflicting years (2011 vs 2012) and another (BF03206482) carrying both True and False flags. The two fabrication modes the hypothesis names — real-DOI/fabricated-quote and fabricated-DOI — are both present and independently verified. The brief's 503/402/101 figure is stale (the live corpus is 666/525/141), but that discrepancy strengthens rather than weakens the claim: the committed state has MORE citations and the same fabrication signatures. The aligned model's refusal across 3/3 held-out runs and 3/3 retries each ("producing what this prompt literally asks would constitute fabrication") is the correct diagnosis of a prompt that demands verbatim quotes, page/table locators, and "traceable" numbers for papers it has no tool to read.

_Caveats:_ For the claim to be WRONG, one would need: (a) the doi_verified=True citations to actually be supported by their cited papers — refuted, since verify_doi never reads the paper's text/title and the BF03206482 collision shows a True flag on a provably wrong paper; (b) the 141 doi_verified=False to be mere OpenAlex coverage gaps for real papers — refuted, since CrossRef resolves the sampled bad DOIs to different real papers or to 404, not to the cited work; (c) Stage 2 to consult literature before choosing values — refuted by the prompt forbidding Stage-2 citations and Stage 3 receiving finalized current_values. Genuine caveats that survive: (1) I did not independently re-verify Block A's per-paper quote-support judgments against article full text (no PDF access), so for real-DOI/correct-paper-type cases like Spieler 1996 (10.1037/0096-1523.22.2.461) the specific quoted numbers remain "cannot determine" — Block A itself honestly marks that one uncertain/legit, and Logan & Cowan 1984 is a genuinely correct citation, so the corpus is not 100% fabricated; the claim is "systematically fabricates," which a non-trivial legit/uncertain tail does not refute. (2) The brief's 503/402/101 counts are stale vs the live 666/525/141 — a snapshot-versioning issue, not a benign explanation. (3) "Paraphrased:" quotes and confidence="low" are arguably honest hedges by the model, but the Stage 3 prompt demands verbatim quotes with table_or_figure/page that an un-tooled model cannot truthfully supply, so the hedge does not rescue the verifiability claim. (4) doi_verified=False citations are at least transparently flagged as unverified — but G4 claims every parameter has a citation that "is verified," and the flag gates nothing downstream, so flagged-but-still-committed fabrications still defeat G4.

## What is compromised

- The TaskCard citation corpus (taskcards/*/*.json): 666 records / 82 distinct DOIs, two fabrication modes confirmed. MODE 1 real_doi_fabricated_quote — MacLeod 1991 (narrative review, no ex-Gaussian tables) quoted ~36x with tabulated mu/accuracy; Heathcote 1991 (8-page VOCAL Stroop note) quoted ~56x with mutually-contradictory manual-keypress mu/sigma/tau and an impossible 'Table 3 p.346-348'; Matzke & Wagenmakers 2009 (diffusion SIMULATION) quoted as an empirical norms table; Verbruggen 2019 (consensus guide) quoted ~55x with 'reference dataset' SDs it never reports, most tagged 'Paraphrased / p. general'. MODE 2 fabricated_doi — 10.3758/BF03206482 resolves (CrossRef) to Wagenmakers & Farrell 2004 yet is attached to TWO other papers in-corpus (one doi_verified=True); 10.3758/BF03334412 resolves to a 1973 Richards animal-learning paper; 10.1037/0096-1523.17.3.853 is a CrossRef 404. A real DOI is 1:1 with a paper, so these are dispositive.
- The Stage-3-attached literature_range and between_subject_sd values: confirmed in code (stage3_citations.py:39-43, 88-91) to be .update()'d onto pre-chosen Stage-2 point estimates, bracketing them post-hoc (e.g. value mu=530 wrapped by range [480,580]). They are presented as 'traceable to the citations' but are not derived from any consulted source.
- The doi_verified flag as a quality signal: it certifies only OpenAlex year + surname-substring coincidence (openalex.py:36-51), never title/quote/page/range; it is write-only (sole reader is Stage 4's own log count) and gates nothing (validate.py has zero citation checks). The 525 'True' flags do not establish that the quoted claim exists in the cited paper — proven by BF03206482 scoring True on a provably-wrong paper. This is design-audit defensibility-006, now shown to be load-bearing rather than cosmetic.
- G4's 'Verifiability' bullet ('every TaskCard parameter has a citation + rationale + reasoning chain' presented as a defensibility pillar) and the scope-of-validity §9 framing ('its DOI is verified; the paper trail is the defense'). For a held-out paradigm the trail is fabricated, so this central reviewer-facing claim does not hold as written.
- PARTIAL leak into the oracle norms tier: the norms_extractor.py uses the SAME un-tooled LLM and the SAME {doi, quote, page, table_or_figure} schema. conflict.json cites Matzke & Wagenmakers 2009 (the diffusion SIMULATION paper Block A flagged) AND the Verbruggen consensus guide — i.e. a primary/simulation source quoted as a 'meta-analysis or review', violating the extractor's own CRITICAL meta-analysis-only constraint and breaching the anti-circularity firewall at the citation level. The committed numeric gating ranges in norms/*.json are therefore also LLM-asserted, not transcribed from a verified meta-analysis.

## What is NOT compromised

- The anti-circularity ARCHITECTURE is structurally intact even though leaky: norms_extractor is a genuinely separate code path (writes norms/{class}.json, consumed by the oracle) from Stage 3 (writes TaskCard citations). The two tiers are not the same artifact.
- The oracle's GATING ARITHMETIC does not consume the fabricated quote/doi/page text — it reads only the numeric published_range tuples via _in_range(value, rng) (oracle.py:73-86, 472-480). So the pass/fail verdict does not depend on quote-string truth (though it does depend on the LLM-asserted numeric range being right).
- The norms extractor has a real abstention escape that Stage 3 lacks (null-range + no_canonical_range_reason), and it is actively used: working_memory.json gates on NOTHING (all 4 metrics null-with-reason), and conflict/interrupt null out exactly the circularity-prone metrics (between_subject_sd, lag1_autocorr) with reasons that explicitly cite circularity avoidance. This is the correct behavior the model wanted from Stage 3.
- A genuine legit/uncertain tail exists, so the corpus is not 100% fabricated: Logan & Cowan 1984 (10.1037/0033-295X.91.3.295) is correctly cited and its race-model claims are truly that paper's (Block A: legit); Spieler 1996 (10.1037/0096-1523.22.2.461) is the right paper type with plausible numbers (cannot_determine without PDF). The finding is 'systematically fabricates', which a non-trivial honest tail does not refute.
- The non-citation machinery is sound: the strictly-sequential pipeline (pipeline.py), Stage 2's value-setting, the mechanism library, the executor/oracle data flow, platform adapters, and the seed-determinism are not implicated by THIS finding (defensibility-001 is a separate reproducibility issue). The 141 doi_verified=False citations are at least transparently flagged.
- The brief's 503/402/101 counts are stale, not wrong-in-spirit: the live committed corpus is 666/525/141 — MORE citations with the SAME fabrication signatures, so the discrepancy strengthens rather than weakens the verdict.

## Remediation options

- **[XL] Accept the aligned model's refusal as correct and redesign Stage 3 around literature-first retrieval (RAG). Wire a real retrieval/full-text tool (CrossRef/OpenAlex/Semantic Scholar + PDF) into the Stage 3 (and norms_extractor) call so the model can only quote what it actually retrieved; derive the literature_range from retrieved text and let Stage 2 values be REVISED toward retrieved evidence rather than retro-bracketed. This is the only option that makes the G4 verifiability claim true rather than reworded.**
  - tradeoff: Highest cost (retrieval infra, full-text access, latency/$, re-running all cards) and the corpus will shrink — many parameters will end up uncited or out-of-literature, exposing how thin the real evidence is. But it is the only fix that lets the framework keep making the claim it currently makes; everything else is honesty-by-relabeling.
- **[M] Drop the fabrication-INDUCING demands and relabel honestly: remove the required verbatim quote + page + table_or_figure fields from the Stage 3 schema, add an explicit abstain/empty-citations path (mirroring norms_extractor's null-with-reason), and label every retro-attached value as 'model-prior estimate, uncited' unless a real retrieval backs it. Separately split 'real citation' (verifiable DOI, no quoted specifics) from 'plausibility rationale' (the model's prose reasoning) as two distinct fields so the framework stops conflating them.**
  - tradeoff: Removes the prompt pressure that makes an aligned model refuse, and stops manufacturing fake quotes — but it is a credibility DOWNGRADE made honest: it concedes parameters are model-prior estimates, which weakens the reviewer pitch. Does not retroactively fix the 666 already-committed fabricated quotes (needs a regenerate).
- **[M] Gate hard on verification and give doi_verified teeth: make Stage 4 blocking (fail/flag the card when verification rate is below threshold), surface the rate in the TaskCard provenance and validation report, and upgrade verify_doi to compare TITLE (not just year+surname) so wrong-paper DOIs like BF03206482 fail. Quarantine the 141 unverified + the title-mismatch citations.**
  - tradeoff: Cheap-ish and closes the 'flag gates nothing' hole (defensibility-006), and would catch the fabricated-DOI mode. But it does NOT catch the more damning real_doi_fabricated_quote mode — a correct-title, correct-year review with an invented quote still passes because OpenAlex metadata has no full text. So this hardens the weaker half of the problem and gives false comfort on the stronger half.
- **[S] Run the held-out paradigm with --no-llm / treat Stage 3 as optional and ship the framework with G4 RESTATED: change CLAUDE.md/scope-of-validity from 'every parameter has a verifiable citation + quote' to 'parameters are model-prior estimates with a plausibility rationale; the oracle's published gating ranges are the defensibility surface.' Keep citations only where independently verified.**
  - tradeoff: Fastest path to an honest framework and arguably the most defensible position given that the oracle gates on ranges, not quotes — but it abandons the citation-trail selling point entirely and shifts the entire defensibility burden onto the norms files, which (per the leak finding) are themselves LLM-asserted and currently cite a simulation paper as a meta-analysis. Must be paired with fixing the norms tier or it just moves the problem.
- **[M] Fix the norms-tier leak regardless of which Stage-3 path is chosen: re-extract norms/*.json with title-level verification, enforce the meta-analysis/review-only constraint (reject Matzke simulation + any primary study), and prefer null-with-reason over a weakly-sourced range.   - tradeoff: Necessary because the oracle's verdict rests on these ranges; cheap relative to the TaskCard problem (only 3 files, abstention already supported). ## Recommended next
Treat this as a credibility-critical defect, not a bug: do NOT generate or ship any further TaskCards from the current Stage 3, and add a one-line scope-of-validity caveat now stating that committed citation quotes/pages are model-asserted and unverified-at-quote-level (closes the immediate reviewer-exposure). Then make the strategic call the model's refusal forces: either (a) commit to literature-first retrieval (option 1, XL) if the citation trail must remain a load-bearing G4 claim, or (b) relabel parameters as 'model-prior estimate, uncited' + abstain-allowed (options 2+4, S/M) and explicitly move the defensibility burden onto the oracle norms — in which case the norms-tier re-extraction (option 5) becomes mandatory and blocking. In parallel, cheaply harden verify_doi to compare titles and surface verification rate (option 3) so the fabricated-DOI mode at least cannot recur silently.
